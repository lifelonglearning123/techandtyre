Mobile Tyre Fitting vs. Traditional Garage — Which is Right for You?

An honest comparison from a mobile tyre fitter who believes the answer depends on your situation — not just our interests.

Mobile Fitting March 2025 By Tech & Tyres, Melksham

We're a mobile tyre fitting company, so you might expect us to tell you that mobile is always better. We're not going to do that. The honest answer is: it depends. What we can do is give you a clear comparison so you can decide for yourself.

For most drivers in Wiltshire — and across Melksham, Chippenham, Trowbridge, and Bath — mobile fitting is the better option for tyre replacements. But there are cases where a fixed garage makes more sense. Here's the breakdown.

Convenience

Mobile fitting wins clearly here. With a mobile service, the fitter comes to you — your driveway, your workplace, or even the roadside if you've had a blowout. You don't rearrange your day around a garage appointment, arrange alternative transport, or sit in a waiting room.

For most tyre replacements, mobile fitting takes 30–60 minutes per tyre at your location. You carry on with your morning; we sort the tyres.

Traditional garages require you to drive in (potentially on a compromised tyre), wait, and collect. For busy professionals or parents, that's a real cost in time.

Pricing

Mobile fitting is typically competitive — and often cheaper. Mobile fitters have lower overheads than fixed garages: no expensive high-street premises, no large team of staff to pay for. Those savings tend to be passed on.

That said, the tyre price itself is similar across the market. Budget, mid-range, and premium tyres are available from both mobile fitters and garages. What you're really comparing is the fitting charge and the value of the convenience.

"Getting new tyres fitted at home or work means no wasted travel time, no waiting rooms, and no arranging a lift. For most drivers, that convenience is worth a great deal."

Range of Tyres Available

This one is closer to even. A well-stocked mobile fitter like Tech & Tyres can source most tyres with 24 hours' notice — including premium brands like Michelin, Bridgestone, Continental, and Pirelli. We can also accommodate next-day fitting for most popular sizes.

Large tyre chains have extensive physical stock on-site, which can be useful if you need something unusual immediately. For standard car tyres in common sizes, the difference is minimal.

Quality of Work

Mobile fitting matches fixed-garage quality when the equipment is right. At Tech & Tyres, we use professional-grade mobile fitting and balancing equipment — the same standard used in fixed garages. We balance all tyres after fitting as standard.

The skill of the individual technician matters more than whether they're working in a garage or from a mobile van. We're RAC accredited, which provides an independent quality assurance standard.

One area where fixed garages have a practical edge: TPMS (tyre pressure monitoring system) resets and certain wheel alignment work. For a straightforward tyre replacement, however, a competent mobile fitter delivers identical results.

When a Traditional Garage Is the Better Choice

We said we'd be honest, so here it is:

  • Complex wheel alignment: Four-wheel laser alignment requires fixed equipment. We can identify an alignment issue, but the correction itself needs a garage with an alignment rig.
  • Multiple mechanical issues: If you need tyres AND a brake overhaul AND a service, a full-service garage is more efficient.
  • Very unusual tyre sizes: Niche sizes may require a larger specialist to source quickly.

When Mobile Fitting is the Obvious Choice

  • You need one or two tyres replaced and don't want the hassle of a garage visit.
  • You're at work and can't easily drop off your car.
  • You've had a blowout and need a roadside replacement.
  • You want a tyre check without committing to a garage appointment.
  • You're a fleet operator and need tyres fitted at your depot, not at a garage across town.

The Bottom Line

For a standard tyre replacement on a passenger car or van, mobile fitting is almost always the better option for Wiltshire drivers. You get competitive pricing, professional-quality work, and you don't sacrifice half a day to get the job done.

For complex mechanical work that goes beyond tyres, a full-service garage earns its place. But for what most people need most of the time, mobile fitting is the smarter choice.
